IMPORTANT NOTES - INTERNAL CIRCULATION ACCOUNTING
================================================================================

‚úÖ WHAT ARE INTERNAL RECIRCULATION FLOWS?
‚Ä¢ Water moving between structures within the SAME area
‚Ä¢ One structure loses water, another structure gains the same amount
‚Ä¢ Net effect on area water balance = ZERO
‚Ä¢ Important for tracking individual dam levels and flow patterns

‚ö†Ô∏è  WHY TRACK SEPARATELY?
‚Ä¢ Dam level predictions: Need to know which dams are gaining/losing water
‚Ä¢ Operational planning: Understanding flow patterns for pump scheduling
‚Ä¢ Water balance validation: Ensure internal flows match between source/destination
‚Ä¢ Regulatory reporting: Some flows must be reported even if internal

üîÑ BIDIRECTIONAL FLOWS EXPLAINED:
‚Ä¢ Some flows can go either direction depending on operations
‚Ä¢ Direction shown as "‚Üî" (two-way arrow)
‚Ä¢ Sign convention:
  - POSITIVE = Flow in the direction shown (FROM ‚Üí TO)
  - NEGATIVE = Flow opposite to direction shown (TO ‚Üí FROM)
  - ZERO = No net flow (balanced or no movement)

üìä WATER BALANCE TREATMENT:
‚Ä¢ For AREA water balance: Internal flows cancel out (don't count as inflow/outflow)
‚Ä¢ For DAM water balance: Internal flows DO count (affect individual dam levels)
‚Ä¢ For MINE-WIDE balance: All internal flows cancel out

üí° OPERATIONAL SIGNIFICANCE:
‚Ä¢ TSF Irrigation: Prevents dust, maintains safe dam freeboard
‚Ä¢ Plant-Dam Exchange: Optimizes water usage, manages water quality
‚Ä¢ Underground Return: Separate category (not internal circulation)

================================================================================
EXAMPLES OF WHAT IS NOT INTERNAL RECIRCULATION:
================================================================================

‚ùå NOT Internal Circulation (these are real inflows/outflows):
‚Ä¢ Underground return (water sent back to mine workings)
‚Ä¢ Inter-area transfers (water moving between different areas)
‚Ä¢ Evaporation (water leaving the system)
‚Ä¢ Consumption (water used and not returned)
‚Ä¢ Seepage (water lost to ground)
‚Ä¢ Spillage (water leaving dam over weir)

‚úÖ IS Internal Circulation (these loop within area):
‚Ä¢ Dam-to-Dam transfers (e.g., NT RWD ‚Üí New TSF)
‚Ä¢ Dam-to-Plant-to-Dam loops (e.g., UG2PCD1 ‚Üî Plant)
‚Ä¢ Treatment-to-Dam returns within same area (e.g., STP ‚Üí Dam)
